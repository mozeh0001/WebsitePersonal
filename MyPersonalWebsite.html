About Me

I’m a BS/MS Computer Science student at USC who wears many hats! On the software side, I enjoy building mobile apps, frameworks, and full-stack websites, while also experimenting with graphics, rendering pipelines, and gameplay programming as every pixel tells a story.

I also love working hands-on with hardware, from microcontrollers and connected IoT devices to PCB design and 3D modeling in CAD. Product design and prototyping let me merge engineering with creativity to bring user-centered ideas to life.

On the research side, I’m diving deeper into machine learning with a growing interest in AGI, NLP, and intelligent systems, and I plan to continue exploring these areas throughout my current time at USC.

Outside of tech, one of my main hobbies is jazz improvisation on the saxophone: trading fours, weaving through blues changes, and slipping bebop lines into my solos while I play the jazz standards. I’m grateful to have studied in person with Alex Hahn, Katie Thiroux, David Crozier, and David Arnay.
