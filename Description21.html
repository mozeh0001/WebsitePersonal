HoF-PoT: Enhancing LLM performance on ARC-AGI tasks.

  • Project: Hint-on-Failure Program-of-Thought (HoF-PoT), a new prompting method for large language models (LLMs).
  • Goal: Improve LLM fluid intelligence on the ARC-AGI benchmarks by providing targeted hints when the model fails mid-reasoning.
  • Datasets: Evaluating performance on ARC-AGI-1 (baseline) and ARC-AGI-2 (frontier challenge).
  • Method: Step-by-step reasoning with “on-failure” hints that guide the model back on track.
  • Baselines: Compared against direct answers, chain-of-thought, self-consistency, and program-of-thought prompting.
  • Evaluation: Measured by accuracy lift, by-category performance, efficiency, and robustness of results.
  • Team: USC research collaboration by David Kalin, Chiddy Golden, Ethan Chiang, Magen Mozeh, Neel Gude, and Ameya Deshmukh.
